Brian Keating

I'm a computer vision engineer who has worked professionally analyzing medical, satellite, and microscopy imagery. Currently, I lead the Computer Vision group as part of Uptake's data science team. Previously, I worked at the University of Chicago's Research Computing Center, and at the Univerty of Hawaii's MRI research group. You can see my resume here and a full (slightly out of date) academic cirriculum vitae here.

The remarkable success of deep learning in computer vision has led me to use convolutional neural nets more often as the backbone of much of my work analyzing satellite imagery. Reading up on the literature for deep learning, it's hard not to be excited about the possibilities for AI in the coming years. I'm particularly interested in computer vision, especially in embodied systems, i.e., robots. My blog will serve as an online notebook for demos, documentation, links, etc., related to personal projects, particularly those in robotics.
